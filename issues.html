Issues

There a several issues surrounding the production of bananas. These issues range anywhere from tropical diseases threatening to kill off the world's most consumed strain of banana to the environmental effects of deforestation necessary for the creation of more banana farms. All told, there are four major issues surrounding bananas. These issues include the environmental, ethical, social, and economic problems created by bananas and their production.

Environmental

The most produced and marketed banana is the Cavendish banana. It is grown in massive quantities in the tropical regions of the world and is sold in massive quantities to countries like the United States of America. Unfortunately, there is a strain of Fusarium Wilt (otherwise known as Tropical Race Four) that is spreading across the banana belt leading to the death of many Cavendish banana plants. This disease could lead to the mass extinction of the entire strain of Cavendish bananas which would wipe bananas as we know them off the food map. The loss of a major strain of bananas due to a tropical disease has already been seen in the Gros Michel banana which was practically wiped out by the Fusarium Oxysporum strain in the 1950's leading to the push in Cavendish banana production.

Ethical

The need to produce more bananas during the 1980s and 1990s in order to supply to a market with increasing demand led to the destruction of much rainforest land in order for it to be used for banana plantations. While this may be seen as more of an environmental issue, it is also ethical in nature as it creates the question of whether we destroy rainforests for food or keep rainforests intact. Obviously, in this situation the answer to that question was to destroy the rainforest in order to plant more banana plants.

Social

As stated, there was a massive boom in the banana industry around the 1980s and 1990s which lead to many issues in and of itself; however, after this boom, there was a decline in the banana market. This drop in the market forced many major banana companies to ramp down production which inevitably lead to the loss of many jobs by people in tropic nations who were working on banana plantations. In the end, the downfall of the market brought some families to extreme poverty.

Economic

Bananas themselves cause a lot of problems for the economies of some of these small island nations. The banana is a massive cash crop for a lot of the smaller island nations in the tropics. When the banana market increases, these nations are in a good place, but when the market falls, the nations and their people often suffer as drops in the market can lead to major drops in exportation which can lead to the fall of banana plantations and the loss of jobs.

Solution

Although many of the issues the banana faces seem daunting, most of them have solutions. As for the environmental issues, researchers must continue closely monitoring the banana population and altering the genetic diversity of bananas in order to keep them safe from vicious diseases. Unfortunately, the diseases will probably never be fully eliminated, so the only logical solution is to mitigate the problem as best as possible. Obviously, destroying entire rainforests to produce more bananas is counterproductive. The best solution is to work on increasing the yield of banana plants so that more fruit can be produced with less land. Until that is possible, it would be best to stop harming natural habitats and end deforestation for bananas. In order to end many of the social and economic issues, the best solution would be to make certain that consumers buy bananas from smaller banana producers as it is often the large companies (Chiquita, Dole and Fresh del Monte) which have monopolized the market that have and will continue to wreak havoc in so many of the smaller island nations. Boycotting their brands would go far in ending so many of the hardships the people and countries experience.
